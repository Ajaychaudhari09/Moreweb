If 2024 was the year of "weird AI spaghetti eating videos," and 2025 was the year of experimentation, **2026 is the year of professional adoption**. The era of glitchy hands and morphing faces is largely behind us. Today, tools like **Sora 2.0** and **Runway Gen-4** are being used not just for memes, but for commercials, music videos, and even B-roll in blockbuster movies.

## The Big Three: A Comparison

### 1. OpenAI Sora 2.0: The Realism King
New features in 2026 include **"Physics Compliance Mode."** In previous versions, liquids didn't splash correctly, and gravity felt floaty. Sora 2.0 apparently simulates basic physics engines *before* rendering pixels.

*   **Best For:** Photorealistic stock footage, architectural visualization.
*   **Drawback:** Still heavily censored/guardrailed.

### 2. Runway Gen-4: The Director's Tool
Runway remains the favorite for creatives because of **Motion Brush 3.0**. You don't just prompt "a car driving"; you paint the path, set the speed, and define the camera lens (e.g., "35mm anamorphic").

*   **Best For:** Music videos, experimental art, precise control.
*   **Drawback:** Subscription costs have skyrocketed.

### 3. Pika Labs 3.5: The Anime Specialist
While Sora chases reality, Pika has doubled down on style. It is the undisputed king of 2D animation, anime style, and stylized 3D. Netflix's latest experimental short was rumored to be 60% Pika-generated.
